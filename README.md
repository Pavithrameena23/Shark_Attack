🦈 Global Shark Attack Trends & Incident Analysis

📌 Project Overview
This project provides a data-driven investigation into global shark attack incidents spanning from the mid-19th century to the present. Using Power BI, I processed a complex dataset covering 909 documented attacks across 64 countries to identify high-risk regions, peak activity times, and the most frequent environmental and biological factors involved in these encounters.

📊 Key Insights
Geographical Hotspots: The USA (317) and Australia (255) lead globally in recorded incidents, followed by South Africa and the Bahamas, as highlighted by the geospatial density map.

Species-Specific Risks: The White Shark is responsible for the highest number of documented attacks (369), nearly doubling the incidents involving the Tiger Shark (196).

Temporal Patterns: Analysis of "Shark Attack by Time" reveals a significant peak during mid-day and early afternoon, specifically between 10:00 AM and 4:00 PM, correlating with peak human recreational water activity.

Demographic Breakdown: There is a stark contrast in victim demographics, with males representing 82.4% (749) of the total cases compared to females at 9.9% (90).

Injury Mapping: The "Total Shark Attack by Body Parts" visual identifies the torso and legs as the primary targets during encounters, providing data that could inform the design of protective aquatic gear.

🛠️ Technical Toolkit
Historical Data ETL: Cleaned and normalized a longitudinal dataset dating back to 1850, handling missing values and inconsistent time formats.

Geospatial Mapping: Utilized interactive bubble maps to visualize the global distribution of incidents across five continents.

Advanced Data Visuals:

Funnel Charts: To rank injury frequency by body part.

Time-Series Charts: To track the exponential increase in documented attacks over the last century.

Column & Bar Charts: For species and country-level benchmarking.

KPI Engineering: Developed high-level cards to provide instant summaries of total attacks, countries involved, and gender distribution.
